Syllabus

Course description & Prerequisites

Basic principles in data analytics; supervised and unsupervised statistical methods; basic deep learning methods for supervised learning; data visualization of standard-size and large size datasets; basic programming language: R and Python.

This course is a required course for the proposed Computational Modeling and Data Analytics Degree. The course sequence is listed at the 3000 level so that the students will have been previously exposed to introductory mathematics (linear algebra, multivariate calculus), a programming language, introductory statistics (basic mathematical statistics), and probability. Students without strong preparation in these will need to invest significant additional time to fill in the gaps.

All class materials are distributed online; for example, you may view most class notes and homework assignments on the Schedule. Canvas is used to report scores from quizzes, homework and the final project.

In-class Quiz

The in-class quiz must be submitted on canvas within an hour after the class. If the quiz is submitted in time, it will be guaranteed to have at least 90. If the quiz is not submitted in time, it will receive a zero score. The two lowest quiz scores will be dropped.

Homework Assignments

Weekly homework assignments will be posted in the on-line Schedule and are due each Friday at the beginning of class starting from the second week, unless otherwise announced in class. Late homework are penalized, and missed homework receive zero scores, but each student's lowest two homework scores are dropped. Homework assignments can be submitted at Canvas or via email to TAs before the due time. Grades will be returned to you.

It is expected that students will read the slides and refereed materials listed in the Schedule . Your work must be legible, include name, and be submitted in a single pdf file. You are expected to put in 6-8 hours of work outside of class. A few of you will do well with less time than this, and a few of you will need more. You must write up your final answers and write your own code independently: copying homework solutions is not allowed.

Final Project

There will be one final project. The final project will begin in the fourth week. A list of available data sets will be released on the forth week. A middle term report on the progress of the project is required. Comments from the instructor and TA will be given for the report. The final report will include a well-written pdf document including (introduction, data visualization, Model & methods, Results) and 15 slides for illustration (made by ppt or latex beamer). You must write up your final report and code independently.

Grades

Your grade will consist of in-class quiz (20%), Homework (40%), and a Final Project (40%).


Quiz 20%
Homework 40%
Final Project 40%

For each categories, the score is ranging from 0-100. For Quiz and Homework, the lowest two scores are removed. The averages of the remaining scores are calculated as the final scores for Quiz and Homework. The total score is the weighted average of scores in all three categories. The total scores in 90-100 are guaranteed at least an A-. The total scores in 80-90 are guaranteed at least an B-. The total scores in 70-80 are guaranteed at least an C-. The total scores in 60 - 69 are guaranteed at least a D-. The lower bound of each interval may be expanded, which depends on the overall performance.
