Syllabus

Course description & Prerequisites

Basic probability theory, Random variables, Expectations, Families of probability distribution functions, Concepts of convergence. Principles of statistical inference, Likelihood based inference, Frequentist and Bayesian paradigms, Sampling distributions and hypothesis testing.

This class requires a solid mathematical (mostly calculus) background: function, limit, continuity, differentiation (e.g. product rule, quotient rule and chain rule), integration, integration techniques such as integration by parts and by substitution, function of two variables, double integrals, infinite series, maxima and minima of functions. Homework 0 is for testing your background. If it seems easy to you then you're probably ready to take this class. Students without strong preparation in these will need to invest significant additional time to fill in the gaps. 

All class materials are distributed on-line; for example, you may view most class notes and homework assignments on the Schedule. Sakai is used to report scores from homeworks and examinations.

Homework Assignments

Weekly homework assignments will be posted on the online Schedule and are due each Tuesday at the beginning of class starting from the second week, unless otherwise announced in class. Late homeworks are penalized, and missed homeworks receive zero scores, but each student's lowest homework score is dropped. Homework assignments can be submitted at class time or via email to TAs before the due time. Grades will be returned to you via Sakai.

It is expected that students will read the book and work problems as required to master the material. Your work must be legible, include name, and be stapled to receive credit. You are expected to put in 6-8 hours of work outside of class. A few of you will do well with less time than this, and a few of you will need more. You may work with other students on the homework problems, but you must write up your final answers independently: copying homework solutions is not allowed.

Exams

There will be one in-class, closed-book midterms exam and a closed-book cumulative final. For each exam, you may bring a scientific calculator and a letter sized cheat-sheet (1-sided for midterm and 2-sided for final) with anything you want written on it. No laptops, netbooks, or cellphones are allowed.

Grades

Your grade will consist of class participation (5%), Homework (15%), a Midterm (30%), and a Final Exam (50%). All grades are reported on Sakai. Late homeworks are penalized, and missed homeworks receive zero scores, but each student's lowest homework score is dropped. Histograms and summary statistics of midterm and final exam scores will be added to the Schedule web page. Brief in-class quizzes will be added if needed.
